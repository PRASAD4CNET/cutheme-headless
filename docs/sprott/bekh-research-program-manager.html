Job Summary

The Dream Legacy Foundation (DLF) and Carleton University Sprott School of Business and Black Entrepreneurship Knowledge Hub (BEKH) is a critical national network of research hubs involved in community-based research, with a mission to improve the lived experiences of the Black community. Through collaboration with a network of multidisciplinary researchers and in linking these with community-based researchers and practitioners and by adopting a regional community-based strategy, the BEKH will co-create and co-generate research that will deliver important knowledge, insights and outcomes that resonates with the local regional needs of Black entrepreneurs, community members and organizations across Canada.

To accomplish this important mission, the BEKH seeks applications for the position of Research Program Manager. Reporting to the BEKH Directors, the Research Program Manager is responsible for ensuring effective operations of the Central Hub which operates out of Carleton’s Innovation Hub and providing oversight to Regional Hub operations. In the Central Hub, this includes responsibility for financial, human, research, and office management and encompasses managing consolidated quarterly reporting to the government ministry of Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada (ISED) on Central and Regional Hub research and community engagement and research activities, research deliverables, communications, and budget consolidation activities.

The Research Program Manager will be based in the Central Hub in Carleton University’s Innovation Hub and will work closely with the DLF, the BEKH Directors, Regional Hub leads, the Sprott School of Business, Innovation Hub staff, the BEKH Advisory Board and the BEKH Research Advisory Committee to ensure that the BEKH fulfils its national mandate and objectives and its commitment to Black entrepreneurs, community members and organizations across Canada.
